The Challenge of Food Insecurity in Our Community

Despite South Africa's agricultural strength, many households in our rural communities, like Tshidimbini near Thohoyandou, still face severe hunger. This isn't just about a lack of food; it's about a lack of access to reliable, affordable, and nutritious food. The root causes are complex and interconnected: Poverty & Unemployment: Many families cannot afford consistently healthy food, especially as prices rise. Climate Change: Unpredictable rainfall, prolonged droughts, and extreme temperatures disrupt traditional farming. Soil Degradation: Years of erosion and nutrient depletion make it difficult to grow crops successfully. Resource Limitations: A lack of funding, tools, seeds, and secure land prevents many from starting their own gardens. Community garden projects offer a powerful, grassroots solution to these deep-seated challenges.
